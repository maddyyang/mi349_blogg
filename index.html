Skincare Basics

October 14, 2024 by Madelynn Yang

Building a skincare routine does not have to be complicated. Start by identifying your skin type: oily, dry, combination, or sensitive. This will help you choose the right products. A good routine starts with cleansing twice a day—morning and night—to remove dirt and oil. If you wear makeup, make sure to remove it fully before cleansing. Exfoliating 1-2 times a week can keep your skin smooth, but be careful not to overdo it, especially if you have sensitive skin. Moisturizing is key, even if you have oily skin. Use a lightweight moisturizer for oily skin or a richer cream for dry skin. Always follow up with sunscreen—a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher is essential to protect against sun damage, which can lead to premature aging and dark spots. As you get comfortable with your routine, you can add treatments like serums or masks to target specific concerns, like acne or dullness. Consistency is key for long-term results, so stick with it!

Pilates

October 15, 2024 by Madelynn Yang

Pilates has become a popular workout for people looking to build strength, flexibility, and mental focus. This low-impact exercise is ideal for all fitness levels, making it a versatile option for beginners and athletes alike. At the core of Pilates is controlled movement. Exercises focus on strengthening the muscles, particularly the core, through slow, deliberate movements. Whether you're using a mat or reformer machine, Pilates helps improve posture, balance, and overall body alignment. One of the biggest benefits of Pilates is that it engages both the mind and body. The workout requires focus on breathing and muscle engagement, making it a great way to reduce stress while exercising. It is also gentle on the joints, which is perfect for people recovering from injury or those seeking a low-impact routine. Over time, consistent Pilates practice can lead to increased flexibility, better core strength, and toned muscles. Plus, it is adaptable—whether you have 10 minutes or an hour, you can easily fit Pilates into your schedule.
